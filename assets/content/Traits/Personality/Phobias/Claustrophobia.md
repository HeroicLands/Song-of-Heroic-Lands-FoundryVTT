Claustrophobia is an intense, irrational fear of confined spaces. People with claustrophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in small or enclosed areas.

### Impulse
Avoiding elevators, tunnels, small rooms, or crowded places. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder
In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic when in tight or crowded spaces.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms
Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts
Persistent and distressing thoughts about being trapped or unable to escape.

### Emotional Distress
Deep feelings of dread, helplessness, and a strong desire to escape when in confined spaces.
