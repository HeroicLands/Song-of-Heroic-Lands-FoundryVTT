Monophobia is an intense, irrational fear of being alone.
People with monophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in situations where they are alone.

### Impulse

Constantly seeking companionship and avoiding activities or situations where solitude is required. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought of being by oneself.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about abandonment, isolation, or lack of security.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread, loneliness, and helplessness when alone or contemplating solitude.
