Thalassophobia is an intense, irrational fear of the sea or large bodies of water. People with thalassophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are near the ocean or large lakes.

### Impulse

Avoiding trips to the beach, boating, swimming, or any activity that involves the ocean or large lakes. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the sight or thought of vast bodies of water. Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about drowning, sea creatures, or being lost at sea.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread and helplessness when near or thinking about the ocean or large bodies of water.
