Gamophobia is an intense, irrational fear of marriage or commitment. People with gamophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in situations where commitment or marriage is a topic.

### Impulse

Avoiding discussions about future plans, commitment ceremonies, or romantic relationships that might lead to marriage. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought of marriage or long-term commitment. Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about the responsibilities and permanence of marriage.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread and helplessness in romantic relationships or discussions of commitment.
