Chiroptophobia is an intense, irrational fear of bats. People with chiroptophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are near bats.

### Impulse

Avoiding caves, forests, or areas where bats are known to reside. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the sight or thought of bats.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about bats causing harm or spreading disease.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread and helplessness when near places where bats might be present.
