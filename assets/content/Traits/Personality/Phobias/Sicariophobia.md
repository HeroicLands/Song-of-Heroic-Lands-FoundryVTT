Sicariophobia is an intense, irrational fear of being murdered or of murder itself. People with sicariophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in situations where they perceive a risk of homicide.

### Impulse

Avoiding dark or deserted areas, staying away from individuals perceived as threatening, or being overly cautious in everyday activities. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought or perception of murder. Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about being murdered or witnessing a murder.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread and helplessness when thinking about violence or in potentially threatening situations.
