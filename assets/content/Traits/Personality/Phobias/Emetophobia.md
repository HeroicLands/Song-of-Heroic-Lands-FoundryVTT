Emetophobia is an intense, irrational fear of vomiting.
People with emetophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or feel the sensation of nausea.

### Impulse

Avoiding certain foods, situations, or places (such as social gatherings or public transportation) that might induce vomiting. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought of vomiting or seeing others vomit. Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about vomiting in public or losing control.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread and helplessness related to the fear of vomiting.
