Ergophobia is an intense, irrational fear of work or the workplace. People with ergophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in work situations.

### Impulse

Avoiding work, procrastinating, or even quitting jobs to escape. Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought or presence of work-related tasks. Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about failure, judgment, or deadlines at work.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread, helplessness, and inadequacy related to professional responsibilities.
