Ptochophobia is an intense, irrational fear of poverty or becoming poor. People with ptochophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in situations related to poverty.

### Impulse
Avoiding areas perceived as economically disadvantaged, obsessively saving money, or refusing to spend on essentials.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder
In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought or sight of poverty.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms
Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts
Persistent and distressing thoughts about becoming destitute or losing financial stability.

### Emotional Distress
Deep feelings of dread and helplessness when faced with financial uncertainty or observing poverty.
