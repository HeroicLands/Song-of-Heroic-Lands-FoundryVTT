Empathy is the capacity to sense and understand the feelings, pain, and motives of others—to imagine oneself in another's circumstances and be moved by their plight. It shapes how a person relates to those around them, influencing their choices in moments of conflict or need. Those with keen empathy find themselves bound to others through genuine feeling; those lacking it move through the world with little regard for the inner lives of their fellows.

### Unfeeling

The unfeeling person is all but deaf to the suffering of others. They observe emotion as one might watch clouds pass across the sky—with detachment and indifference. They are not necessarily cruel; they simply do not register the weight of another's tears or despair. Other people exist mostly as objects or obstacles in their path.

### Remote

Remote individuals sense that others have feelings, but those feelings seem distant to them, almost theoretical. They understand the concepts of sorrow and joy in their minds but feel no answering resonance in their own breast. They may manage a functional courtesy but lack the warmth that comes from genuine connection.

### Average

Those of average empathy grasp the feelings of those near to them and are stirred by extreme suffering, though their engagement fluctuates with circumstance and familiarity. They feel genuine sadness at a friend's loss yet remain largely unmoved by the hardships of strangers.

### Understanding

Understanding folk carry a keen sensitivity to the emotional lives of others. They readily perceive pain and joy alike, and are moved to action by what they perceive. They listen well, remember kindnesses, and feel genuine connection to those they know. Their words often comfort, and their presence is sought.

### Sensitive

The sensitive person's empathy is so acute that they feel the emotional weather of those around them almost as their own. They absorb others' suffering deeply and are sometimes overwhelmed by it. This may make them extraordinarily compassionate healers and counselors, or it may leave them brittle and exhausted from constant exposure to the world's pain.

### In Practice

When a child weeps at the roadside, the unfeeling traveler rides past. The remote one slows, uncertain what is expected. The average traveler asks what is wrong, offers modest help, then continues. The understanding soul sits and listens, offers shelter and food, remembers the child's name. The sensitive traveler weeps alongside the child and feels their loss for weeks after.
