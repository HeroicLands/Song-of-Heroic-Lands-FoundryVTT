Ordinary fear is common and passes without lasting effect. **Traumatic fear** —
brought on by the threat of death or injury, arcane forces, or supernatural
occurrences — instead demands a **Fear Test**: a test against **Will**.

> **Critical failures: `CF0` vs `CF5`.** A d100 roll whose **least-significant
> digit is 0 or 5** is a _critical_ — a critical success when the roll succeeds, a
> critical failure when it fails. Where the outcome depends on _which_ critical
> failure, **CF0** (last digit 0) is the more severe and **CF5** (last digit 5)
> the less severe. Several trauma tests — Fear, [Morale](Morale.md), and
> [the Pall](The_Pall.md) — draw this distinction.

| Result              | State                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **CF0** — Catatonic | Unaware, unable to act, and Helpless (melee attackers score a Critical Success Ignore). At the end of each turn the victim makes another Fear Test; any success improves the state to Terrified, otherwise they retest. Gains **+2 PSY** ([Psyche Stress](Psychological_Condition.md)). |
| **CF5** — Terrified | Rooted in place; may respond in combat only with Block or Dodge. On the next turn the victim must flee the source at full Move (or Pass if they cannot). After **ten minutes** clear of the source they become Steady. Gains **+1 PSY**.                                                |
| **MF** — Afraid     | May respond in combat only with Block or Dodge, and must flee the source at full Move on the next turn (or Pass). Once clear of the source, a Fear Test on MS/CS makes them Steady (else retest each turn); after **one minute** clear they become Steady regardless.                   |
| **MS** — Steady     | Immune to **this** source of fear (other sources still take effect).                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **CS** — Brave      | Immune to this source, and gains **+20** to all Fear and Morale tests for five minutes.                                                                                                                                                                                                 |

When several fear sources are present, only the **most severe** (most-failed)
state affects the victim.
