(https://www.kelestia.com/). It is designed so that you may play using those rules with this system. However, note that no content from HMK is present in this work; all content is unique to SoHL.  It is anticipated that additional modules will be published in the future that will provide the skills, spells, monsters, sunsigns, and other content specific to Kethira.

The rules presented here are basic rules necessary to run a game.  For more detailed rules, including those for trekking and other abilities, please consider purchasing HMK from Kelestia.com.

# Core System
The SoHL rules resolve uncertainty through a unified system of d100 and d10 rolls. Every test in the game—swinging a sword, casting a spell, crafting a horseshoe, persuading a lord—uses the same fundamental procedures described below.

## Dice

The system uses two dice types: d100 (percentile, generated by rolling two d10s) and d10. The d100 drives all primary skill and attribute tests. The d10 serves Secondary Rolls and various special-purpose checks.

## Key Concepts

- [[Success_Tests|Success Tests]] — the foundation of all resolution. Roll d100 against Mastery Level; determine success level (CF, F, S, CS).
- [[Opposed_Tests|Opposed Success Tests]] — when two characters act against each other. Both test simultaneously; highest success level wins. Victory Degrees measure the margin.
- [[Success_Value_Tests|Success Value Tests]] — for sustained or complex tasks. Combine skill Index with a Success Test modifier to produce a graduated quality result.
- [[Secondary_Mastery|Secondary Mastery]] — how ancillary skills and attributes influence a primary test, either as a modifier to EML (Secondary Modifier) or as an accompanying d10 check (Secondary Roll).
- [[Body_Structure|Body Structure]] — the two-tier anatomy (body parts and body locations) that governs where blows land, how armor protects, and how injuries impair function. Includes strike accuracy and the success-margin hit location system.

## Design Principles

The system emphasizes graduated outcomes over binary pass/fail. A character who succeeds critically produces meaningfully better results than one who barely succeeds, and a critical failure carries consequences beyond simple non-achievement. This granularity extends to opposed situations, where the margin of victory (measured in Victory Degrees) often determines the severity of the outcome.

Skills improve through use. Characters who test their abilities in play develop them organically, reflecting the principle that practice and experience—not abstract experience points—drive growth.
