A small fruit pie, slightly cooled or freshly baked, makes a quick breakfast or reeve's payment for a morning's work. The filling—apple, plum, or berry—rests beneath a thin pastry crust, just sweet enough to relieve hunger. Such pies spoil within a day or two and are best bought warm from the baker's counter.
