Large meaty fish, white-fleshed and mild, dried or salt-cured for long trade voyages and winter storage. Coastal towns and fishing villages trade cod by the barrel; inland merchants stock salt-cod for meatless days and for feeding workers and garrisons. Dried cod is rehydrated in pottage, flaked into bread, or boiled with root vegetables for a meal that sustains without spoiling.
