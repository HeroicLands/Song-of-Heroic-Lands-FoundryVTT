This meat pie is baked with a sealed crust of fine wheat flour, holding a filling of seasoned meat, onions, and rendered suet. Richer than the common version, it appears at tavern counters and merchant feasts. A single pie provides sustenance for half a day or shares among two light eaters. Kept wrapped in cloth, it remains edible for several days before drying out.
