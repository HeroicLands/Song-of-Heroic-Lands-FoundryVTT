Dressed waterfowl with dark, rich meat and a layer of fat beneath the skin, prized for its flavor by wealthy tables. A merchant or estate-holder serves duck roasted whole or jointed; the fat renders for cooking and the bones make strong broth. The meat is more robust than chicken and spoils more slowly, keeping a day or two fresh before salt or smoke becomes necessary.
