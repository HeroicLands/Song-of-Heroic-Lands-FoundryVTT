Twin flat blades of polished bone or hardwood, fastened at the heel by a leather thong or iron pivot, struck together in hand to mark rhythm and accent. Players working bone clappers develop a distinctive rattle; those favoring wooden pairs achieve a sharper, brighter crack. Common at festivals, harvest celebrations, and temple festivals where percussion heightens the festive mood.
