A portable drum of shallow frame with a single or double hide head, played by striking with the palm and fingers rather than mallets. Musicians in minstrel bands favor hand drums for their quick, bright voice and portability; they sit well on the lap or hang at the player's side. The variety of tones—from sharp cracks to muted thuds—suits both solemn processions and joyful dances.
