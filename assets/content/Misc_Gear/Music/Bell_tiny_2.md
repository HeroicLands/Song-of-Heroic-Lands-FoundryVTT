A small iron or pewter bell fitted with a loop or leather strap, light enough to hang from a girdle or woven into the hem of a skirt. The sharp, thin voice cuts through soft spaces—useful for goatherds, cooks signaling from kitchen to hall, or a servant alerting a master. Often several are suspended together to compose a modest chime.
