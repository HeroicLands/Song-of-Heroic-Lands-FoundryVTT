Six bone or wooden dice, each cut with squared faces and inked with numbers one through six. Dice are used in taverns, barracks, and camps for gaming and wagering; soldiers and merchants carry sets to pass time on long journeys or trade roads. A matched set of six is a mark of fairness and quality, more trustworthy than odd lots that invite accusations of cheating.
