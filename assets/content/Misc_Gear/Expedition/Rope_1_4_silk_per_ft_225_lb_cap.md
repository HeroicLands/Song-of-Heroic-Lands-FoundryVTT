Silk rope is spun from the continuous filament of cocoon fibers, twisted tight and smooth. Far lighter and stronger than hemp, it resists rot and moves freely through a carabiner or pulley, making it prized by climbers, riggers, and thieves who spare no coin for advantage. A single hand-span of silk holds a man’s full weight with room to spare, though the knots that secure it must be set with care.
