# What Is an Injury?

An Injury represents a specific wound sustained by a Being — a sword cut to the arm, a blunt trauma to the head, a burn on the torso, and so on. Injuries are the primary consequence of combat in SoHL and each one tracks its own healing timeline. Unlike afflictions (which are ongoing conditions), injuries represent discrete physical damage to a specific part of the body.

# Where It Appears

Injuries are located on the Being **Trauma** tab. Injuries are usually created automatically as a result of the combat resolution pipeline when an attack lands, but they can also be added manually  to represent falls, environmental hazards, or other sources of harm.

# Additional Properties
Along with the [[Item_Base|Standard Item Properties]], the following properties also appear in the **Properties** tab:
- **Injury Level** — the severity of the wound, which determines the mechanical penalties and healing difficulty.
- **Healing Rate** — how quickly the injury heals, influenced by the
  character's attributes and any medical treatment received.
- **Aspect:** The aspect of the damage, one of _Blunt_, _Edged_, _Piercing_, or _Fire_.
- **Is Treated:** Whether or not the wound has been treated. Untreated wounds heal worse than treated wounds.
- **Is Bleeding:** Whether or not there is significant bleeding associated with the wound.  Bloodloss from wounds can cause weakness and eventually death if left untreated.
