Theatrics is the art of performance and disguise—the ability to assume a role, alter one's apparent identity, and sustain a convincing persona through voice, mannerism, and bearing. Unlike Guile, which distorts facts while the speaker remains ostensibly themselves, Theatrics involves wholesale transformation of how one presents to the world. Applications range from stage performance and storytelling to espionage and infiltration. A skilled practitioner can adopt accents, mimic social classes, and project emotions they do not feel, though Intrigue remains the primary defense against even the most polished deception.
