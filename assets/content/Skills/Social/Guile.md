Guile is the art of deliberate deception—convincing others of something the speaker knows to be false. Unlike Theatrics, which involves assuming a false identity or persona, Guile operates through distortion of facts while the deceiver remains ostensibly themselves. Its applications range from a merchant misrepresenting wares to a spy feeding disinformation, from a litigant misleading a court to a card player bluffing opponents. Deception is resisted through Intrigue, the complementary skill of detecting falsehood.
