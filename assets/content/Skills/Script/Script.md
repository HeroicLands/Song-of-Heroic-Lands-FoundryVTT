Script represents literacy in a specific writing system—the ability to read, write, and interpret written text. While spoken language may be acquired naturally through immersion, the written form must typically be taught, making full literacy a mark of education that distinguishes the learned from the merely articulate. Different scripts may encode the same spoken language, and mastery of one writing system does not automatically confer the ability to read another.
