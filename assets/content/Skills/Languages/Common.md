Common is the shared trade language spoken across most of the known world, serving as a lingua franca between peoples of different native tongues. While regional dialects and accents vary considerably, the core vocabulary and grammar remain mutually intelligible from one realm to the next. Mastery reflects not merely fluency but the depth and nuance of expression one can achieve—from the halting phrases of a foreigner to the eloquent rhetoric of a trained orator.
