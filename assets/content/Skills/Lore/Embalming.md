Embalming is the specialized knowledge of preparing a corpse for burial according to the rites of a particular faith or culture. Techniques may include evisceration, immersion in preservative solutions, wrapping the body, and packing cavities with herbs and aromatic substances. A skilled embalmer can significantly delay natural decomposition, and the art also encompasses a rudimentary form of forensic examination—studying a body to determine approximate cause and time of death. Climate and environmental conditions play a significant role in how quickly preservation must be undertaken.
