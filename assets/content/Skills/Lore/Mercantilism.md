Mercantilism represents knowledge of trade, commerce, and the economic principles that govern the exchange of goods and services. A skilled merchant can appraise the value of commodities, negotiate favorable terms, identify market trends, and navigate the complex web of tariffs, guild regulations, and trade routes that define commercial life. The skill also encompasses basic bookkeeping and an understanding of currency systems, weights, and measures across different regions.
