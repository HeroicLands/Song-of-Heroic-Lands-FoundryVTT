Folklore represents a character's knowledge of their homeland's culture, history, and traditions. Scholars typically organize this body of knowledge into broad categories: geography, history, the natural and supernatural world, myth and legend, poetry and literature, and social customs. A Folklore test may determine whether a character can recall a specific piece of information or gauge their general depth of understanding about a broad topic. Knowledge of foreign lands diminishes sharply with distance, making well-traveled individuals and those with access to libraries particularly valued as sources of information.
