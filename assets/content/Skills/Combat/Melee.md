Melee governs the effective use of hand-held weapons in close combat—swords, axes, maces, polearms, and all other arms wielded in direct confrontation with an opponent. It measures not just the ability to swing a weapon but the tactical awareness to exploit openings, the footwork to maintain advantageous positioning, and the instinct to read an opponent's intentions. Proficiency develops through training and live combat experience, with raw physical attributes of coordination and agility forming the foundation upon which technique is built.
