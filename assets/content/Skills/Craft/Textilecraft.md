Textilecraft encompasses the production of fabrics and finished textile goods—spinning fiber into thread, weaving cloth on a loom, dyeing materials, and sewing garments and other items. The craft covers everything from coarse homespun to fine linens and wools, and a skilled textile worker understands the properties of different fibers, the techniques of various weave patterns, and the chemistry of dyes. Textilecraft is among the most widespread of trades, practiced at some level in nearly every household and at an artisanal level in every settlement of any size.
