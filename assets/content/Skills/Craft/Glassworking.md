Glassworking is the craft of producing glassware—bottles, goblets, flasks, vials, windowpanes, and decorative objects—through the skilled manipulation of molten glass. The process requires intense heat, steady hands, and an understanding of how the material behaves at various temperatures. Finished products range from simple utilitarian vessels to exquisite stained-glass windows and precision lenses, with the crafter's ability determining both quality and complexity.
