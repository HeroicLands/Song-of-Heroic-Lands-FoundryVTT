Fletching encompasses the crafting of bows, crossbows, arrows, and quarrels—the full range of projectile weaponry. Though the term technically distinguishes the arrow-maker from the bow-maker, in practice most crafters do both. Production requires access to a workshop, appropriate materials, and considerable time, with the quality of the finished product varying according to the crafter's skill and the tools available. Exceptional work can produce masterwork weapons of superior range and accuracy, while poor craftsmanship yields unreliable arms that may fail at critical moments.
