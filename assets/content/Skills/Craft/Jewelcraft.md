Jewelcraft encompasses gem-cutting, goldsmithing, silversmithing, and the creation of fine ornamental metalwork. Practitioners produce rings, amulets, necklaces, bracelets, brooches, and occasionally more elaborate pieces like tiaras and crowns. The craft demands precision, patience, and an eye for beauty, as both the setting and any mounted gemstones contribute to the finished piece's value. Cut gems may also be worked independently as adornments or traded as compact stores of wealth.
