Ceramics is the craft of shaping clay into durable objects hardened by fire—pots, bowls, tiles, bricks, and decorative sculpture. A skilled ceramicist can throw forms on a wheel in minutes, though the lengthy process of drying, firing, and glazing determines the final quality. The craft applies basic principles common to a wide variety of functional and artistic items, making it one of the most broadly useful of the artisan trades.
