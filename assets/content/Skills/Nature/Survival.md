Survival encompasses the practical knowledge needed to endure in the wilderness—finding shelter, locating water, foraging for edible plants, navigating by natural signs, and coping with the hazards of weather, terrain, and wildlife. It is the skill of the frontier scout, the lost traveler, and anyone who must sustain themselves far from the comforts of civilization. While some basic survival instincts are nearly universal, true competence in the wild demands determination, keen senses, and hard-won experience.
