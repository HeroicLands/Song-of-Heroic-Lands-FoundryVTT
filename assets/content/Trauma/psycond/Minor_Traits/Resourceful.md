Resourcefulness is the gift of finding swift and clever solutions to difficulties. A resourceful person does not merely accept the constraints of circumstance; they work within them, discovering unexpected paths where others see only walls. In a world where merchants lose caravans to bandits and smiths must forge steel without the right ore at hand, such ingenuity is worth its weight in silver.

### Quirk

At its best, resourcefulness appears as practical cunning. A resourceful miller discovers he can use a damaged millstone by splitting it and using the halves for separate tasks. A resourceful traveler arrives at an inn where provisions are scarce and fashions a hearty broth from kitchen scraps and foraged herbs. These are not heroic acts—they are the quiet work of people who have learned to see possibility where others see waste.

**Example:** A castle steward who, during a siege when the grain stores threaten to run dry within a fortnight, begins rationing supplies more sparingly, repurposes stored root vegetables in ways that stretch the harvest, and teaches the garrison to make filling meals from foods previously deemed fit only for the swine—thereby extending their provisions for twice the expected duration.

### Impulse

Resourcefulness becomes problematic when it turns manipulative or obsessive. A person may begin to contrive solutions nobody asked for, seeing problems everywhere and compulsively "fixing" situations that were not truly broken. They grow unable to accept straightforward answers, always searching for a hidden complication or a clever workaround when the simple path would suffice. This can veer into deception—borrowing a tool they were not permitted to use, or altering someone else's work to "improve" it without permission.

**Example:** A talented craftsman whose compulsive need to "improve" every task leads him to meddle with tools and projects that are not his own, earning the resentment of his fellow artisans and the distrust of his master.

### Disorder

At its worst, resourcefulness becomes manic scheming. The person cannot rest for always plotting some new solution, real or imaginary. They spin elaborate plans that collapse under scrutiny, borrowing and re-borrowing beyond their means, fabricating problems so they may solve them, earning a reputation as a charlatan or a fool. They may become paranoid that others are not being "creative enough" and begin to undermine their authority, stealing materials, falsifying records, or engaging in outright fraud—all in the name of solving some crisis they alone perceive.

### Physical Symptoms

Constant movement, tinkering, handling of objects. Hands are rarely still. Such people often appear disheveled, marked by grease stains and broken fingernails from perpetual meddling. Sleep is broken by bursts of renewed activity.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Endless loops of problem-solving that refuse to settle. "If I only tried this combination... what if I approached it from another angle?" The mind latches onto difficulties—real or imagined—and cannot release them.

### Emotional Distress

Restlessness and frustration when unable to act. Anxiety that without their cleverness, all around them will collapse into chaos. A deep insecurity masked by the need to constantly prove their value through problem-solving.

**Example:** A cook whose drive to improve every meal becomes obsessive, experimenting with forbidden herbs and unusual combinations that poison the lord's family, insisting all the while that his innovations were simply misunderstood strokes of genius.
