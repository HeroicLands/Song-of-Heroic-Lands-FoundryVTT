Vigilance is the habit of watchful attentiveness to potential dangers and problems before they arise. A vigilant person does not sleepwalk through the day; they attend to the small signs that others miss—the fraying rope, the horse that limps, the stranger whose questions seem too knowing. In a world where bandits waylay the careless and a single overlooked detail can turn fortune to ruin, vigilance is a gift that often saves lives.

### Quirk

At its best, vigilance is a protective awareness that benefits everyone within its sphere. A vigilant captain notices the dark clouds gathering on the horizon and orders the caravan to shelter before the storm arrives. A vigilant mother observes her child's fever and fetches the physician before the illness deepens. A vigilant night-watch sees the figure attempting to steal into the castle and sounds the alarm. Such people are the guardians of their communities; their attentiveness prevents catastrophe.

**Example:** A castle guard who maintains a constant and careful watch over the surrounding landscape from the battlements, alert for any signs of approaching enemies, maintaining regular patrols and teaching others to recognize threats, thereby ensuring the safety of all the inhabitants.

### Impulse

When vigilance becomes excessive, it transforms into exhausting hypervigilance. The person begins to see threat everywhere, even in harmless situations. They cannot relax for a moment, convinced that the instant they lower their guard, disaster will strike. They begin to anticipate problems that do not exist, creating preventative measures for imaginary dangers. Their constant scanning for threat makes those around them anxious; the vigilant person's worry is contagious. They may become controlling, insisting on rules and precautions that others find oppressive. Sleep becomes difficult; they remain poised on the edge of wakefulness, ready to respond to danger at any moment.

**Example:** A steward whose compulsive need to anticipate every possible problem leads him to impose increasingly strict rules on his household—insisting that all doors be barred at dusk, that no strangers be admitted under any circumstances, that every meal be tested before the lord eats—creating an atmosphere of such oppressive caution that the household begins to seethe with resentment.

### Disorder

At its most pathological, vigilance curdles into paranoia. The person cannot distinguish between real and imagined threats. Every shadow contains an enemy; every innocent remark is a secret message of hostile intent. They may engage in constant checking behaviors—verifying locks repeatedly, searching rooms for hidden assassins, testing the loyalty of those closest to them through elaborate deceptions. Some become violent, lashing out preemptively against threats that exist only in their mind. Others become so consumed by vigilance that they neglect basic self-care, driving themselves toward collapse through exhaustion and the impossible demand of eternal watchfulness.

### Physical Symptoms

The body is perpetually tensed for action, muscles knotted and strained. Such people often exhibit tremor, particularly in the hands. Sleep is severely disrupted; they may go nights without rest, remaining awake to maintain watch. The eyes are often reddened and tired; the face is drawn.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Constant loops of threat-scanning: "What could go wrong here? What am I missing? Is that person a danger?" A relentless internal monologue that interprets neutral stimuli as warnings and assigns threat to innocent occurrences.

### Emotional Distress

Persistent anxiety and a sense that safety is impossible. Exhaustion from the impossible demand of eternal vigilance. Shame at the inability to feel at rest, coupled with blame directed outward ("The world is genuinely dangerous; I am simply more aware than others").

**Example:** A knight whose constant vigilance against threats—both real and imagined—has led him to execute innocent servants on suspicion of treason, to refuse all counsel from his advisors as potentially conspiratorial, and finally to collapse entirely, his years of hypervigilance manifesting as complete paranoid break during which he believes his own household has risen in rebellion against him.
