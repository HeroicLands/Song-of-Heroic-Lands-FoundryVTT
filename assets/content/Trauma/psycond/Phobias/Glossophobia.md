Glossophobia is an intense, irrational fear of public speaking or speaking in front of a group. People with glossophobia may experience a range of symptoms when they think about, see, or are in speaking situations.

### Impulse

Avoiding any form of public speaking, including presentations, speeches, or even classroom participation.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 1 from the source of fear.

### Disorder

In addition to trait intensity behaviors: Overwhelming fear or panic at the thought of speaking publicly.
Abberance (ABE) increases by 3 (instead of 1) from the source of fear.

### Physical Symptoms

Rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, nausea, or dizziness.

### Intrusive Thoughts

Persistent and distressing thoughts about being judged or failing during public speaking.

### Emotional Distress

Deep feelings of dread and helplessness when faced with the prospect of speaking in front of others.
