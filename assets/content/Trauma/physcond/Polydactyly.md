Polydactyly is a condition wherein a person is born with extra digits—additional fingers on a hand or additional toes on a foot. The extra digit may be fully formed and functional, partially developed, or rudimentary. To some, such a person is marked by fortune; to others, they bear a sign of supernatural touch or divine displeasure. In villages where superstition clings like morning mist, a polydactyl child's fate often depends entirely on whether the first person to see them interprets the mark as blessing or curse. ### Trait At its mildest, polydactyly is an asset. A person born with six functional fingers on each hand possesses an advantage in certain crafts—weaving, fishing, playing complex instruments. The extra digits may require no special care; they function much as the others do. The person moves through childhood without shame, their difference unremarked or accepted as simply the way they are fashioned. In some villages, such people are valued for their utility and their unusual nature, viewed as blessed by luck or touched by the water-spirits. **Example:** A child born with six functioning fingers on each hand, whom the villagers regard as fortunate, believing the extra digits mark them as touched by good fortune or gifted with an otherworldly connection, depending on local lore—some viewing the condition as a sign of good luck, others as evidence of mystical ancestry. ### Impulse As the person matures, they begin to feel the weight of being different. The extra digit—whether on hand or foot—marks them immediately as unusual. If the digit is poorly formed or non-functional, it becomes an object of focused attention and comment. The person may attempt to hide the extra digit by binding it, covering it with wrappings, or avoiding situations where their hands or feet are exposed. They develop a hypervigilance about their appearance, reading threat into every glance. Superstitious relatives may begin to whisper that the extra digit is a mark of curse rather than blessing, feeding the person's own growing shame. Some become defiant, flaunting the difference as proof of their uniqueness, even as deep insecurity grows beneath the bravado. **Example:** A youth whose two extra toes on one foot make him visibly different, leading him to refuse to remove his boots even in the privacy of his home, to avoid swimming or bathing in public, and to develop an intense defensiveness about his appearance, ready to lash out at any comment about his condition. ### Disorder When the extra digit is severely malformed, non-functional, or chronically infected, the condition becomes a source of acute distress. The person may refuse to be seen, developing severe social anxiety and avoiding public places entirely. Some come to believe that the extra digit marks them as fundamentally wrong, unfit for love or normal life. They may pursue dangerous "remedies"—attempting to amputate the digit themselves, using caustic substances to burn it away, or seeking out unscrupulous practitioners who make false promises of correction. In extreme cases, a person may become so convinced of their own wrongness that they abandon basic self-care, neglect the infection or pain, or harbor thoughts of self-harm as an escape from their visible difference. ### Physical Symptoms The extra digit is clearly visible, varying in degree of development. If partially formed, it may appear as a stub or flap of tissue. In well-developed cases, the extra digit is fully functional but visibly supernumerary. Chronic infection is common where the extra digit is cramped in regular footwear or catches on tools and cloth. Swelling, tenderness, and restricted movement are frequent complaints. ### Intrusive Thoughts Constant awareness of being different, looping thoughts about what others see when they look at the extra digit: "Are they disgusted? Do they think I am cursed? Will anyone ever accept me?" A compulsive need to hide or cover the digit. ### Emotional Distress Profound shame about visible difference coupled with helplessness—the knowledge that concealment is temporary, that the truth of the body cannot be hidden forever.
